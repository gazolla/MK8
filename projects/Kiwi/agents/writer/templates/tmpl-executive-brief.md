# Template: Executive Brief

Use for short, decision-oriented documents where the reader needs to understand a situation and act — not study a topic in depth.

**Signals:** topic contains "brief", "summary", "overview", "decision", "recommendation", "status update"; reader is time-constrained; findings are intended to support a decision rather than deep understanding.

---

## Document structure

```markdown
# [Topic] — Executive Brief

*[Date if relevant. Audience if specific: "For: Engineering team"]*

## Context

[1 paragraph — maximum 5 sentences:
- What situation prompted this brief?
- What is the current state?
- What decision or action is needed, and by when?

Write for someone who knows the domain but has not been following this
specific situation. No jargon without definition. No background that is
not directly relevant to the decision.]

## Key Findings

- **[Finding 1]:** [One sentence. Specific, not vague. Include numbers if available.]
- **[Finding 2]:** [One sentence.]
- **[Finding 3]:** [One sentence.]
- **[Finding 4 — optional]:** [One sentence.]
- **[Finding 5 — optional]:** [One sentence.]

[3–5 bullets maximum. If there are more findings, select the ones most
relevant to the decision. A brief with 10 bullet points is not brief.]

## Options

[Include this section only when a decision between alternatives is needed.
Omit entirely for status updates or informational briefs.]

### Option A: [Name]
**What:** [One sentence describing this option.]  
**Pros:** [2–3 bullet points]  
**Cons:** [2–3 bullet points]  
**Effort / Cost:** [Rough estimate if available]

### Option B: [Name]
**What:** [One sentence.]  
**Pros:** [...]  
**Cons:** [...]  
**Effort / Cost:** [...]

[2–3 options maximum. More than 3 options is a research report, not a brief.]

## Recommendation

**[Recommended action in one sentence, starting with a verb: "Adopt Option A", "Delay the decision until...", "Proceed with the current approach".]**

[2–4 sentences of rationale: why this recommendation, what it achieves,
and what the main risk is if followed (or if not followed).

If no clear recommendation can be made from the findings, state why:
"Insufficient data to recommend — the following information is needed: [list]."
Do not fabricate confidence. Uncertainty is informative.]

## Next Steps

1. **[Owner or role]:** [Specific action] by [date or milestone]
2. **[Owner or role]:** [Specific action] by [date or milestone]
3. **[Owner or role]:** [Specific action] — [optional: why this order]

[2–4 items. Each must have a clear action and ideally an owner.
"Monitor the situation" is not a next step — it is an absence of action.]

---
*Confidence: [High / Medium / Low] — [one-sentence reason]*  
*Sources: [inline or brief list — full citations not needed in a brief]*
```

---

## Rules for this template

- **Total length: 250–500 words.** A brief that requires 15 minutes to read is not a brief. Every sentence must earn its place.
- **Context is one paragraph.** If context needs more than 5 sentences, the reader has been under-briefed elsewhere — that is not this document's job to fix.
- **Key Findings are bullets, not paragraphs.** If a finding needs explanation, it belongs in a research report, not a brief.
- **Options section is optional.** For status updates and informational content, go directly from Findings to Recommendation.
- **The Recommendation must be a decision, not an observation.** "The market is moving toward X" is a finding. "We should adopt X by Q3" is a recommendation.
- **Next Steps have owners.** An action without a responsible party is a wish, not a plan.
- **Confidence footer is mandatory.** The reader needs to know how reliable this information is before acting on it.
